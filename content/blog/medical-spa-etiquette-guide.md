Medical spas operate at the intersection of healthcare and hospitality, and the expectations around client behavior reflect both of these environments. Understanding the etiquette of a medical spa visit helps you have a more positive experience and ensures the staff can provide you with the best possible care.

## Be Honest in Your Health History

Your health history intake form is not bureaucratic paperwork — it is clinical information that directly affects which treatments are safe and appropriate for you. Disclose all current medications including supplements and over-the-counter drugs, any history of cold sores if you are considering treatments in the lip area, any recent procedures or treatments elsewhere, any autoimmune conditions, and any history of keloid scarring.

Omitting information because you think it is irrelevant or because you are concerned it will disqualify you from a treatment you want can lead to complications. A qualified provider will use this information to protect you, not to refuse you care unnecessarily.

## Arrive Without Makeup for Skin Treatments

If you are visiting for any facial skin assessment or treatment, arrive without makeup. Your provider needs to see your actual skin condition to assess it accurately and to begin your treatment without having to spend time removing products first. Foundation and concealer hide the coloring, texture, and characteristics that inform your treatment plan.

## Be Clear About Your Goals and Budget

Medical spa consultations are most productive when you are clear about both what you want to achieve and what your budget allows. Being specific about your primary concerns allows the provider to prioritize appropriately and recommend what will make the most meaningful difference for you. Being transparent about your budget prevents recommendations that exceed what you are prepared to spend and allows the provider to suggest the highest-impact treatments within your range.

## Ask Questions Before Treatment Begins

The consultation is the time to ask every question you have, no matter how basic it seems. Once a treatment begins, the time for questions has passed. Ask about the products being used, the expected results, the potential risks, the recovery process, and what you should do if you experience any concerns after leaving.

A reputable medical spa welcomes informed clients who ask thoughtful questions. A practice that seems impatient with questions or that discourages you from asking is not operating with the client's best interest as its priority.

## Follow Pre-Treatment Instructions Carefully

Many medical spa treatments come with pre-treatment instructions that directly affect both your safety and your results. Common requirements include avoiding blood-thinning medications and supplements before injectables, avoiding retinoids before certain peels or laser treatments, avoiding sun exposure before laser treatments, and arriving with clean skin for facial treatments.

Following these instructions is not optional. Ignoring them can lead to increased bruising, adverse reactions, or compromised results that could have been avoided.

## Respect the Clinical Environment

A medical spa is a professional healthcare environment. Arriving on time for your appointment respects both your provider's schedule and the schedules of other clients. Bringing small children to procedures where your attention needs to be on the treatment is generally not appropriate. Phone calls during treatments are disruptive to the provider and can lead to movement that affects precision.

## Post-Treatment Communication

If you experience anything unexpected after a treatment — unusual swelling, prolonged redness, pain, or anything that concerns you — contact the medical spa promptly rather than waiting to see if it resolves on its own. A reputable practice wants to know about these occurrences and has protocols for addressing them. Early communication about any post-treatment concerns leads to better outcomes than delayed reporting.

## Mutual Respect as the Foundation

The relationship between a medical spa client and their provider is a professional healthcare relationship that benefits from the same mutual respect and honest communication that characterizes any effective medical relationship. A client who is honest about their health history, communicates their goals and concerns clearly, follows pre and post-treatment instructions, and engages respectfully with their provider will consistently receive better care and better results than one who approaches the interaction transactionally. The investment in this relationship, built over multiple visits with a trusted provider, is one of the most valuable aspects of ongoing medical spa care.

The best outcomes in medical aesthetics consistently come from a combination of choosing the right provider, having realistic expectations, following pre and post-treatment instructions diligently, and committing to the consistency of care that allows treatments to produce their full cumulative benefit. Every investment you make in finding the right practice and building a trusted professional relationship pays dividends across every treatment that follows.
