Acne and the scarring it leaves behind are among the most common and most distressing skin concerns people bring to medical spas. While many people spend years managing acne with over-the-counter and prescription topicals with variable success, medical spa treatments offer more targeted and often more effective solutions for both active acne and the textural damage it causes.

## Treating Active Acne at a Medical Spa

For clients with active acne, medical spa treatments address the condition through several mechanisms that topical products alone cannot achieve.

Chemical peels using salicylic acid, a beta-hydroxy acid that is oil-soluble and can penetrate into the pore, effectively clear congestion and reduce inflammation. A series of regular salicylic acid peels produces meaningful improvement in active breakouts and pore congestion over time.

Blue light photodynamic therapy uses specific light wavelengths that target and destroy Propionibacterium acnes, the bacteria responsible for inflammatory acne. When combined with a photosensitizing agent applied before the light treatment, the effectiveness is enhanced. This approach is particularly useful for clients with moderate to severe inflammatory acne who have not responded adequately to topical treatments.

Extraction during a medical facial removes existing congested pores and comedones professionally, reducing the bacterial load and preventing existing congestion from developing into inflammatory breakouts. Professional extraction performed correctly is significantly more effective and less traumatic than self-extraction.

Prescription skincare that may be accessible through a medical spa's physician oversight, including higher-strength retinoids and topical antibiotics, can also be more effective than over-the-counter options for active acne management.

## Treating Acne Scarring

The textural damage that acne leaves behind, including rolling scars, boxcar scars, and icepick scars, represents a separate and distinct treatment challenge from active acne. Several medical spa treatments produce meaningful improvement in acne scarring.

Microneedling is one of the most effective treatments for rolling and boxcar acne scars, stimulating collagen production that gradually fills in and remodels depressed scar tissue. A series of four to six treatments spaced four to six weeks apart produces progressive improvement over several months.

Fractional laser resurfacing, using devices like fractional CO2 or erbium lasers, creates controlled microscopic columns of thermal injury in the skin that trigger a powerful collagen remodeling response. This produces more significant improvement in deeper or more pronounced scarring than microneedling alone, though with more recovery time required.

Chemical peels at medium depth improve superficial acne scarring and post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, the reddish or brownish discoloration that follows healed breakouts.

Subcision is a minor in-office procedure in which a needle is passed under a depressed acne scar to release the fibrous bands tethering it to the deeper tissue, allowing the scar to rise. It is particularly effective for rolling scars and is sometimes combined with microneedling or filler for enhanced results.

## Managing Expectations

Acne scarring, particularly deep icepick scars or fibrotic scarring, responds more slowly and less completely to treatment than other skin concerns. Realistic expectations involve significant improvement rather than complete elimination of scarring. Most clients with moderate to severe scarring achieve their best results through a combination of treatments over multiple sessions, rather than a single treatment solution.

A thorough consultation with a provider who has specific experience in acne scar treatment will give you the most accurate picture of what is achievable for your specific type of scarring and skin.

## A Comprehensive Approach Produces the Best Results

The clients who achieve the most significant improvement in both active acne and acne scarring at medical spas are almost always those who combine professional treatments with appropriate medical-grade home care under professional guidance. Treating acne as a multifaceted condition that requires attention both in the treatment room and at home every day, rather than expecting in-office treatments alone to resolve it, is the mindset that produces the most satisfying and lasting outcomes.

The best outcomes in medical aesthetics consistently come from a combination of choosing the right provider, having realistic expectations, following pre and post-treatment instructions diligently, and committing to the consistency of care that allows treatments to produce their full cumulative benefit. Every investment you make in finding the right practice and building a trusted professional relationship pays dividends across every treatment that follows. Working with a provider who understands your specific goals, assesses your situation thoroughly, and communicates honestly about what is achievable creates the foundation for outcomes that consistently meet or exceed your expectations over the long term of your aesthetic care relationship. The field of medical aesthetics continues to evolve with new technologies and refined techniques that expand what is safely and effectively achievable without surgery, and a provider who stays current with these advances offers you access to the most effective options available for your specific concerns at every stage of your aesthetic journey.
