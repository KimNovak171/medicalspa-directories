Kybella has become a well-established treatment at medical spas for clients who are bothered by fat beneath the chin, the area commonly called a double chin. As the only FDA-approved injectable specifically designed for this purpose, it offers a non-surgical alternative for a concern that is otherwise difficult to address without liposuction. Here is what you need to know before considering treatment.

## What Kybella Is

Kybella contains deoxycholic acid, a naturally occurring molecule in the body that is involved in the breakdown and absorption of dietary fat. When injected into the submental fat beneath the chin, deoxycholic acid destroys the fat cell membranes, causing the fat cells to break down and be gradually eliminated by the body's natural metabolic processes.

Once fat cells are destroyed by Kybella, they do not regenerate, making the result permanent in those specific cells as long as significant weight gain does not occur.

## Who Is a Good Candidate

Kybella is appropriate for adults who have moderate to severe fat beneath the chin and who are bothered by this concern. The ideal candidate has good skin elasticity in the neck and chin area, meaning the skin will retract well as the fat volume is reduced. Clients with significant skin laxity in the neck and chin may not achieve optimal results from Kybella alone because reducing the fat volume without addressing the skin can sometimes make laxity more apparent.

A thorough consultation with your provider that includes an assessment of the fat volume and skin quality in the treatment area will determine whether Kybella is the most appropriate approach for your specific anatomy.

## The Treatment Experience

Kybella is administered as a series of small injections distributed across the submental fat area. Most clients receive between 20 and 50 injection points per session depending on the amount of fat present. A topical numbing agent or ice is typically applied before treatment to reduce discomfort.

The most significant aspect of the Kybella experience is the recovery period, which is more substantial than many other injectable treatments. Swelling, bruising, numbness, and firmness beneath the chin are expected and can be pronounced for one to two weeks following each treatment session. Some clients experience swelling that lasts three to four weeks.

## How Many Sessions Are Needed

Most clients require two to four treatment sessions to achieve their desired result, with sessions spaced at least one month apart to allow full recovery between treatments. Some clients with larger fat deposits require up to six sessions.

Results develop gradually as the body processes and eliminates the destroyed fat cells over the weeks following each treatment. The final result of a complete Kybella series is typically visible three to four months after the last session.

## Realistic Expectations

Kybella produces real and lasting reduction in submental fat for appropriate candidates. The result is a more defined jawline and chin profile that can be quite meaningful for clients who have been bothered by this concern. However, the recovery involved with each session is something candidates should be prepared for, and the multiple session requirement means the full treatment timeline spans several months.

Clients who prefer minimal recovery time may find that other approaches such as CoolSculpting for the submental area or surgical liposuction suit their circumstances better. Discussing all available options during a consultation helps ensure you choose the approach that best fits your goals and your tolerance for downtime.


## Weighing the Options

Kybella is a legitimate and effective treatment for submental fat reduction, but it is not the only option and may not be the best option for every individual. CoolSculpting for the chin area, surgical liposuction, and in cases where skin laxity is the primary concern, skin tightening treatments, all have their own appropriate indications. A thorough consultation that evaluates all available options and recommends the approach that best fits your specific anatomy, your goals, and your tolerance for downtime will produce the most satisfying outcome.
