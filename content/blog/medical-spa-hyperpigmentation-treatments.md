Hyperpigmentation — the appearance of darker patches or spots on the skin caused by excess melanin production — affects a significant majority of adults to some degree. Whether it appears as sun spots, melasma, post-inflammatory marks from acne, or general uneven tone, hyperpigmentation is one of the skin concerns that responds most consistently to medical spa treatment. Understanding what causes different types of hyperpigmentation and what treatments are most effective for each helps you approach treatment more strategically.

## Types of Hyperpigmentation

Not all hyperpigmentation is the same, and identifying the type you have is the first step toward effective treatment.

Sun spots, also called age spots or solar lentigines, are discrete flat brown spots that develop from years of cumulative UV exposure. They are most common on the face, hands, and other areas of frequent sun exposure.

Melasma presents as larger, more diffuse patches of pigmentation typically appearing on the cheeks, forehead, upper lip, and chin. It is strongly influenced by hormonal factors including pregnancy and oral contraceptive use, and by UV exposure. Melasma is one of the most challenging types of hyperpigmentation to treat because it tends to recur without strict ongoing sun protection and maintenance.

Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation is the dark discoloration that follows skin inflammation from acne, eczema, injury, or other inflammatory conditions. It is more common and more pronounced in darker skin tones.

## IPL and Laser Treatments

Intense pulsed light and laser treatments are among the most effective options for discrete sun spots and mild to moderate uneven pigmentation. These energy-based treatments target melanin in the skin, breaking up pigment deposits that the body then clears. A series of treatments typically produces significant improvement in sun damage and irregular pigmentation.

For melasma, laser and light treatments require more caution. Heat-based treatments can trigger a rebound increase in pigmentation in melasma-prone skin if not performed carefully with appropriate parameters. Not all medical spas have the expertise to treat melasma safely with energy-based devices, and a consultation with a provider experienced specifically in melasma treatment is important.

## Chemical Peels for Pigmentation

Medium-depth chemical peels effectively address superficial to moderate hyperpigmentation by removing the outer skin layers along with the excess melanin they contain and triggering the production of new, more evenly pigmented skin. A series of peels combined with a prescription home skincare routine produces consistent improvement in most types of hyperpigmentation.

## Prescription Brightening Agents

Prescription-strength hydroquinone, available through a medical spa's physician oversight, is the most effective topical brightening agent available and produces more significant results than any over-the-counter alternative. It works by inhibiting the enzyme responsible for melanin production. Prescription retinoids used alongside hydroquinone enhance results by accelerating skin cell turnover.

## The Role of Sun Protection

No treatment for hyperpigmentation will produce lasting results without rigorous daily sun protection. UV exposure drives melanin production and will cause treated pigmentation to return regardless of how effective the treatment was. Broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher applied daily is non-negotiable in any effective hyperpigmentation treatment protocol.

Your medical spa provider should emphasize sun protection as a fundamental component of your treatment plan. A provider who recommends treatments for pigmentation without stressing the importance of daily SPF is not providing complete guidance.

## Patience and Consistency Produce Results

Hyperpigmentation is one of the most treatable skin concerns at a medical spa, but it is also one that requires patience and consistency to address effectively. Most types of pigmentation respond to a well-designed treatment protocol that combines professional treatments with appropriate prescription home care and strict sun protection, but the improvement unfolds over weeks and months rather than in a single session. Clients who commit to the full protocol and maintain sun protection diligently consistently achieve the most significant and lasting improvement.
