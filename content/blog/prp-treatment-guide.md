Platelet-rich plasma treatments have grown in popularity at medical spas over the past decade, offering a biologically based approach to skin rejuvenation and hair restoration that uses the body's own growth factors rather than synthetic substances. Understanding what PRP actually is, what it can realistically achieve, and what the treatment involves helps you evaluate whether it is appropriate for your specific goals.

## What PRP Is

Platelet-rich plasma is derived from the patient's own blood. A small blood sample is drawn and then spun in a centrifuge that separates the blood components by density. The process concentrates the platelet-rich plasma, which contains significantly higher concentrations of growth factors and bioactive proteins than normal blood.

These growth factors, including platelet-derived growth factor, transforming growth factor, and vascular endothelial growth factor among others, play important roles in tissue repair, collagen stimulation, and cell regeneration. When introduced into the skin or scalp at therapeutic concentrations, they trigger biological processes that improve tissue quality and stimulate the growth of new cells and structures.

## PRP for Skin Rejuvenation

When PRP is injected into the skin or applied topically immediately after microneedling creates channels for deeper penetration, it enhances the skin rejuvenation effects of these treatments. The growth factors stimulate collagen and elastin production, improve skin texture and firmness, and accelerate healing.

PRP is most commonly used in combination with microneedling for this purpose, with the PRP applied to the skin immediately after microneedling to allow the growth factors to penetrate through the micro-channels created by the needles. The combination produces more significant collagen stimulation than either treatment alone.

Results from PRP skin treatments develop gradually over weeks to months as the collagen remodeling process continues. A series of three treatments spaced four to six weeks apart is typically recommended for optimal results.

## PRP for Hair Loss

PRP injections into the scalp have become a widely offered treatment for androgenetic alopecia, the most common form of hair loss in both men and women. The growth factors in PRP stimulate the hair follicles, prolonging the active growth phase of the hair cycle and potentially reactivating dormant follicles.

Research on PRP for hair loss shows meaningful improvement in hair density and thickness in many patients, though results vary considerably between individuals. PRP for hair loss is most effective for patients in the early to moderate stages of hair loss where follicles are thinning but still present rather than permanently lost.

A typical PRP hair treatment series involves three to four sessions spaced one month apart, followed by maintenance treatments every three to six months to sustain results.

## What to Expect During Treatment

A PRP appointment begins with a blood draw of approximately 20 to 60 milliliters depending on the treatment being performed. The blood is then processed in the centrifuge for approximately ten minutes. For facial treatments, microneedling or micro-injections are performed and the PRP is applied or injected. For scalp treatments, the PRP is injected directly into the scalp using a fine needle.

Mild redness, swelling, and tenderness at the treatment areas are expected and typically resolve within one to three days.

## Evaluating Realistic Results

PRP treatments produce real biological effects, but the degree of improvement varies between patients and is not as dramatic or predictable as some other medical spa treatments. An honest consultation with a provider who reviews the current evidence and sets appropriate expectations for your specific situation is essential before proceeding.


## PRP as Part of a Comprehensive Plan

PRP treatments produce their best results when integrated into a comprehensive skin care or hair restoration plan rather than used as a standalone solution. For skin rejuvenation, PRP combined with microneedling and supported by a medical-grade home skincare routine produces more significant results than PRP alone. For hair loss, PRP combined with appropriate medical management of the underlying hair loss condition and complementary treatments produces the most meaningful improvement. A provider who assesses your complete situation and designs a plan that uses PRP appropriately within a broader treatment strategy will consistently produce better outcomes than one who offers PRP as an isolated intervention.
